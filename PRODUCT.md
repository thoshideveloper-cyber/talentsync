# Product

## Register

product

## Users

HR recruiters and talent acquisition professionals at Indian organizations — typically processing 5–30 job descriptions per week. They operate in compliance-sensitive environments where a badly-worded JD creates legal exposure under India's Labour Code 2025. They are competent professionals who want a tool that feels as smart and precise as they are, not one that talks down to them or buries them in bureaucratic UI. They are often showing the tool to hiring managers or senior leadership and need it to look credible.

## Product Purpose

TalentSync is a JD Intelligence Platform that normalizes, compliance-checks, and corrects job descriptions before they are posted. It surfaces seniority mismatches, bias flags, missing pay ranges, and regulatory violations — then gives the recruiter AI-assisted tools to fix them. The output is a clean, audit-grade JD with a provenance trail. Success looks like: a recruiter processes a JD in under two minutes, is confident it will pass compliance review, and can export a corrected document + audit report without any back-and-forth with legal.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Trustworthy. Human.

The tool should feel like a skilled compliance colleague who is thorough and serious about getting things right, but is still approachable — not a cold regulatory system. The tone is confident and direct, never condescending or excessively cautious. The visual language should project authority without being corporate-gray dull.

## Anti-references

- **Workday / SAP SuccessFactors** — dated, enterprise-gray, form-over-task. Recruiters complain about tools that look like this daily.
- **Generic SaaS blue** — default Tailwind/shadcn palette. Blue primary on white, zero brand identity. What TalentSync currently is; must move past it.
- **Notion / Linear clones** — over-minimal, blank-page aesthetic. The product handles real compliance risk; it should not look like a note-taking app.
- **ATS dashboard wallpaper** — gradient hero metrics, glowing donut charts, drop-shadow card grids with big numbers for their own sake. The metrics here carry real meaning; they should be presented with precision, not showmanship.
- **AI-generated looking** — the uncanny valley of AI design: overly-rounded cards, gradient text, glassmorphism, sketchy illustrations, warm-cream backgrounds. Nothing that makes a first-time user think "this was assembled, not designed."

## Design Principles

1. **Verdict first** — the most important signal (pass/fail, flag count, quality score) appears at the top of every screen, never buried. Recruiters make decisions fast; the UI earns that speed.
2. **Signal over noise** — every visual element on screen is earning its place. No decorative badges, no eye-candy that delays the task. Density is a feature; clutter is not.
3. **Earned trust at every screen** — audit-grade output demands audit-grade presentation. Seniority evidence, compliance citations, and provenance are displayed with the same care a legal team would expect.
4. **Warmth in precision** — the tool handles serious compliance risk but is used by humans every day. Typography, color, and copy should feel human and considered, not bureaucratic. Warmth lives in the details.
5. **Consistent vocabulary** — buttons, labels, affordances, and states look the same across every surface. Recruiters build muscle memory; the tool should reward that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 body text, 3:1 large text)
- All interactive elements focusable with visible ring style
- Motion respects `prefers-reduced-motion`
- Color is never the sole carrier of meaning (flags use icons + text, not just red/green)
